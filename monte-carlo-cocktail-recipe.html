Whiskey Sip'd

Monte Carlo

This is the sexier, lesser known cousin of the Manhattan.

The peppery rye dances with the spiced sultry benadictine and is married by the smooth slaps of peychaud’s bitters which is then excited by the oils of a fresh lemon peel.

This wonderful cocktail is one of my all-time favorites to drink and make for customers.

If you’re reading this as a bartender and this isn’t in your repertoire yet, please do yourself and your customers a favor and start making these!

You can adjust the amount of benadictine you use based on the heat of the whiskey.

Any Rye will be ok here, but I suggest choosing a Rye you really like because it will be the star of this cocktail.

    Ingredients
  • 2 oz Your Favorite Rye Whiskey
  • .50 oz Benadictine
  • 4 Strong dashes of Peychaud's Bitters
  • Lemon Peel for Oil and Garnish
    Tools
  • Mixing Glass
  • Jigger
  • Bar Spoon
  • Coupe style or Nick an Nora style Cocktail Glass
    Method
  1. Fill your Coupe with ice and soda water to chill it down.
  2. Pour the Whiskey, Benadictine and Peychaud’s into a Mixing Glass.
  3. Fill with ice.
  4. Stir with your Barspoon for 50 revolutions.
  5. Dump out the ice water from the coupe.
  6. Using the Julep Strainer, strain the cocktail into the now chilled coupe.
  7. Using a fresh lemon peel, express the oil into the cocktail and around the glass, making sure to rub the peel side around the rim of the glass.
  8. Rest the curled peel onto the rim of the glass.
    9. Enjoy!
