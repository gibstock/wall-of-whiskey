Heaven Hill Bottled-In-Bond

(click here to jump to tasting notes)

December 30, 2019
By Wall of Whiskey

Heaven Hill Distillery

We've all heard of/had some Heaven Hill in our lives.

They're the powerhouse distillery behind some of the whiskey greats from Evan Williams (a personal favorite back in my highschool debauchery days) to Elijah Craig (a current favorite) and more.

It all began in 1933, post prohibition, where the Shapira brothers decided it was time to get in on the whiskey game and invested in a distillery in Bardstown and eventually purchased it outright.

Whiskey Where?-House

Prohibition and the war effort leveled most of the distilleries and even after the repeal, it took a long time for Bourbon Whiskey to get back on the market.

The war effort was a drain on distilleries' resources as they were required to make distillate for use in military accessories without compensation.

Because ya know, Bourbon must be aged, so naturally it just took time.

The Payoff

But with that patience, the Shapiras' released Old Heaven Hill Bottled-In-Bond Whiskey and it quickly became the highest selling whiskey in Kentucky.

Check this out if you're curious about what "Bottled-In-Bond" means...

It's a Family Affair

To this day they continue to be family-owned and operated which I think is fantastic and adds to the overall experience of enjoying their whiskey.

Tasting

The Look:
Dark Amber, Burnt Sienna, Sexy Legs.

The Smell:
Sweet Apple Peels, Nutmeg, Toasted Marshmallow.

The First Sip:
The first thing I noticed was baking chocolate.

It's a new sensation to me but a great descriptor of something that has a wonderful bitter dark chocolate smell without the creamy sugary sweetness of the milk chocolate you'd find in a candy bar.

Then a wonderful tonic-like taste that reminded me of bark from an oak tree.

Next you noticed the wonderful nip at the tip of your nose that is a low heat.

Second Sip:
The second dip in I got a strong Hazelnut taste, followed by cloves, baked apple and a hint of cinnamon.

The Remainder:
A wonderful heat remained on my tongue as well as the warming winter spices, particularly clove.

Conclusion:
The whole experience was incredibly balanced, from the rich color to the warming holiday spices. I absolutely loved it and will be revisiting this next chance I get!

I Would buy this as a gift for any Whiskey Enthusiast and one for yourself while you're at it!!
