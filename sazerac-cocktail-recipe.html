Whiskey Sip'd

Sazerac

A Sazerac is an old cocktail hailing from New Orleans. Some people get turned off from trying this elite cocktail when they read Absinthe in the ingredients, but the hint of Absinthe is what makes this cocktail really special. As with any culinary achievement, these flavors hit all the notes in perfect harmony.

Don’t skip any of the steps. I know sometimes it seems things like rubbing a citrus peel on the rim of the glass is arbitrary, but I assure you it is not. All these things add to the complexity of a great cocktail.

    Ingredients
  • 2 oz Rye Whiskey
  • .50 oz Simple Syrup (1:1)
  • 1/8 oz Absinthe
  • 4 Dashes of Peychaud's Bitters
  • Lemon Peel Garnish
    Tools
  • Mixing Glass
  • Jigger
  • BarSpoon
  • Julep Strainers
  • Chilled Rocks Glass
    Method
  1. Chill the rocks glass by filling it with ice and soda water.
  2. Add the Rye, Simple Syrup, Peychaud’s and Absinthe into the Mixing Glass.
  3. -Side note about the Absinthe: For as long as I can remember, I’ve always added the Absinthe to the cocktail glass as a rinse and then dumped it out. This still works perfectly fine as it is the classical way of making the drink. Until my friend and Rocket Scientist Level Bartender, Chad Brown, hipped me to adding the Absinthe to the mixing glass and leaving it in! It’s simple ingenuity like this that keeps the cocktail world revolving and evolving. –
  4. Stir for 40 revolutions. You should see condensation starting to form on the bottom of the Mixing Glass and it should be cold to the touch.
  5. Dump the ice water from the chilled Rocks glass.
  6. Using the Julep Strainer, strain the liquid into the chilled rocks glass.
  7. Squeeze the lemon peel (outer side facing the cocktail) to express the oil all over the cocktail. You should see a fine spray of delicious hit the surface of the cocktail and create a glorious glimmer. Rub the rim all the way around with the same side of the peel. Twist the lemon and place it on the rim of the glass, making sure not to drop the peel inside the cocktail.
    8. Enjoy!
