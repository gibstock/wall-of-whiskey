My Favorite Recipes

  • Sazerac

    Some people get turned off from trying this elite cocktail when they read Absinthe in the ingredients, but the hint of Absinthe is what makes this cocktail really special.

    As with any culinary achievement, these flavors hit all the notes in perfect harmony.

  • Brown Derby

    Thinking I had created a unique masterpiece, I started pushing the cocktail like I was some sort of cocktail wizard!

    Until my pride came crashing down when a very apt customer says to me: “Oh you mean a Brown Derby?”

  • The Manhattan

    The Manhattan.

    If you’ve not heard of a Manhattan and you consider yourself an adventurer of the imbibe, go out and get one now.

  • Old Fashioned

    I think this is one of the most important cocktails out there, and the easiest to learn.

    A few simple ingredients for your home bar and you've got unlimited killer Old Fashioneds.

  • Daiquiri

    Day or night, hot or cold, this perfect cocktail will relax and energize you.

    One of the freshest!

  • Hot Toddy

    Feeling under the weather? Or just don't feel like freezing your hands off with a frosty beverage?

    Try one of these. Sick tested, bartender approved.

  • Monte Carlo

    This is the sexier, lesser known cousin of the Manhattan.

    The peppery rye dances with the spiced sultry benadictine and is married by the smooth slaps of peychaud’s bitters which is then excited by the oils of a fresh lemon peel.

  • Basil Gimlet

    This is a fun twist on a traditional gimlet.

    The egg-white can be omitted from this recipe, but please keep the basil and absinthe!

    It’s a great winter holiday cocktail.
