The Manhattan

The Manhattan. If you’ve not heard of a Manhattan and you consider yourself an adventurer of the imbibe, go out and get one now.

Even though this cocktail is straightforward in its three-ingredient design, it is truly one that has infinite forms by simply replacing the elements.

The original Manhattans (if memory serves correct) was a cocktail with more vermouth than whiskey, a true lunch-time cocktail.

Then it became a 50-50 split, which is a great cocktail given the right selection of Whiskey and Vermouth.

Then it swung to almost no vermouth due to the availability of less potable vermouths, which is still widely served today.

Hey, I’m all for a cold slug of whiskey, but that ain't a Manhattan!

The ratio that I favor is one higher in whiskey, but also just under half as much vermouth. With the wide variety of vermouths on the market, you can make a great balanced Manhattan easily.

    Ingredients
  • 2 oz Your Favorite Bourbon
  • .75 oz Good Sweet Vermouth (Cocchi is my preference)
  • 4 Dashes of Angostura Aromatic Bitters
  • Brandied Cherry for garnish
    Tools
  • Mixing Glass
  • Jigger
  • BarSpoon
  • Julep Strainers
  • Coupe, Martini, or Rocks Glass
    Method
  1. Depending on how you want it (Up: In a Coupe or Martini Glass without ice, On the Rocks: in a Large Rocks Glass with a Large Ice Cube) chill the appropriate glass with ice and soda water.
  2. Add the ingredients to the Mixing Glass.
  3. -A note about using bitters: When I say “Dash” I want you to give it some umph! You know when you get the last few squirts of your Sweet Baby Ray’s BBQ Sauce (Is the Boss) and you must put your shoulder into it to whip that stuff from the bottom of the bottle to the neck? Do it like that. Not only are you adding bitters to the mix, you’re also aerating it, releasing the flavors trapped inside. Think of it as a micro-midair-decanting. I might start saying “4 Slaps of Bitters” just to get the point across.
  4. Fill with ice and stir consistently for 50 revolutions.
  5. Dump the ice water from the chilled cocktail glass.
  6. Using the Julep Strainer, strain the liquid into (or over the cube) the chilled glass.
  7. Garnish with a brandied cherry (not a vibrant neon red cherry if you can avoid it)
    8. Enjoy!
