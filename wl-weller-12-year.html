Wall of Whiskey

WL Weller 12 Year

Whiskey Sip'd

(click here to jump to tasting notes)

December 5, 2019
By Wall of Whiskey

What is "wheated" bourbon?

I had an idea of what it meant, based on what I knew about bourbon: Bourbon must contain at least 51% Corn in it's mash bill. So that means there's a good amount of wheat in there instead of the typical Rye. However I wasn't exactly sure what the wheat brought to the table.

The Tastes of Bourbon

In a general sense, I know that corn brings a sweetness that forms the overall taste structure to bourbon, and rye brings the wonderful pepper note and holiday spices.

Whatabout-da wheat dough?

It turns out wheat is generally considered 'flavor-neutral', but allows for more of the notes from the bourbon (and the barrel it's aged in) to shine through.

I imagine it's like the Vodka of flavoring: It doesn't have it's own strong identifying taste profile, but aids in bringing out other flavors. Think of it as a supporting cast role.

Another thing I believe it brings is a viscous 'mouth feel'. We've all sipped on some Grey Goose right? Right? Well that unique mouth feel is due mostly to the Wheat it's made from (and other stuff too).

Well if you didn't know (don't worry, neither did I), now you know what a "Wheated" bourbon is and why you should like it!

I'm sure you've all heard of the ever-sought after "Pappy Van Winkle" collection, well that's a wheated bourbon. So if you want the Pappy experience at a fraction of the cost, check out some WL Weller 12 year!

(Cue end of Reading Rainbow book review sound: Dun-uhhh Duh!)

Tasting

The Look:
You know when you open a can of black olives? That sheen? That's what I'm seeing here. There's almost a light-oily quality to this light amber liquid.

The Smell:
The very second thing my nozzle detected (after the ethynol) was a tangy green apple. Followed wonderfully by a caramel smell. Now that takes me back to Old Sac or Apple Hill, being a little tyke eating a drippy caramel apple with 4 missing teeth, one of which is stuck in said apple.

The First Sip:
This starts off with a real high burn, makes ya feel alive! Then I tased something familiar that took my brain a few minutes of searching: Pops cereal! It must be the mix of higher wheat with the corn that gave me this sensation. They say that wheat ads a great depth of flavor to whiskey, and if this is any indicator, I agree!

Second Sip:
When I went back in, I got something unique, and I think the term for it might be 'tobacco'. It reminded me of the earthy taste on some wines, but with a slightly more stingy aspect.

The Remainder:
This whiskey washed completely around my mouth and stuck around the underside of my tongue. It pracitally coated the roof of my mouth and up into my nasal passages (similar to chugging a soda and burping: the way the bubbles gather to the tip of your nose).

Conclusion:
I'm blown away. The heat remained throughout the sips (I'm learning I like the heat) and it was not overly sweet. It made me salavate, which made me want more, which made me salavate... I find this to be one of my favorites, so far. I will definitely suggest this as a great bourbon to have the full "bourbon experience".

Will drink again!!
