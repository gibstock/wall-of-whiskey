Basil Gimlet

This is a fun twist on a traditional gimlet.

The egg-white can be omitted from this recipe, but please keep the basil and absinthe!

It’s a great winter holiday cocktail. Absinthe is now easily purchased at your local BevMo.

Any time you're using a fresh herb as a garnish give a good SPANK by holding it in the open palm of one hand and smacking it with your other.

This lightly breaks the cells and releases the wonderful oils from within.

These are the small details I was talking about. It makes all the difference.

Egg white doesn't add any flavor at all to the cocktail, it just gives it a viscous mouth-feel. Similar to drinking the foam of a cappuccino

    Ingredients
  • 1.5 oz Gin of your choosing
  • .75 oz fresh squeezed lime juice
  • .50 oz Simple Syrup (1:1)
  • .50 oz Egg-white
  • Basil leaves for shaking and garnish
  • .25 oz Absinthe for rinse
    Tools
  • Shaking Tins
  • Jigger
  • Fine Strainer
  • Hawthorne Strainers
  • Chilled Coupe
    Method
  1. Start by chilling down the glass with ice and soda water (you’re noticing a pattern here)

  2. Add the Gin, lime juice, simple syrup, egg-white (if using) and 4-5 nice sized fresh basil leaves to the small Cheater Tin.

  3. Fill with ice and shake it like smacked your grandma!

  4. Don’t shake it too long, just long enough to feel the tin get cold, but you don’t want to over dilute.

  5. -Skip to step 10 if you’re not using egg-white-

  6. Crack open the tins so that the ingredients are all in the large tin.

    Using the Hawthorne Strainer, strain the contents into the smaller cheater tin and discard the left-over contents in a sink/trash or what have you.

  7. Re-seal the shaker tins making sure to get a real tight one.

    Shaking with egg tends to build up pressure and if the tins aren’t properly sealed, you’ll have egg-white flying everywhere!

  8. Using both hands to keep the tins together, shake it HARD! Hard like you’re earning a dollar for each shake being harder than the last!

  9. When the tin goes silent, you’re done.

  10. Dump the ice-water from the chilled coupe and pour a little absinthe into the glass.

    In a swirling fashion, coat the inside of the glass all the way up to the rim of the glass.

    I like to do this by continuously twisting the glass, starting with the absinthe in the middle and working it to the rim by tilting it, until you have the glass completely upside down and the absinthe has poured out.

    Do this over a sink or into a glass or directly into someone’s mouth. No need to waste the absinthe!
  11. Crack open the tins for a final time and “Double Strain”into the chilled, absinthe-rinsed coupe.

    -Double strain means straining first from the tin with your Hawthorne strainer and then again through the fine strainer to clear any unwanted bits/pulp/debris-
  12. If you made it with the egg-white, make sure to give the fine strainer a few taps to get all of the cocktail out.
  13. Take a nice-looking basil leaf, give it a good SMACK to wake it up, rub it all around the rim and let the leaf float beautifully on the center of the cocktail.

    If made with egg-white it should float on top of the foam.
    14. You’ll love it!
