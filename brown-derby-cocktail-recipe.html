Brown Derby

This is a cocktail that I was making before I knew the name of it! A customer was asking me for a personalized cocktail during a busy rush, and almost spitefully I through together the ingredients that were closest to me. She ended up loving it! Thinking I had created a unique masterpiece, I started pushing cocktail like I was some sort of cocktail wizard! Until my pride came crashing down when a very apt customer says to me: “Oh you mean a Brown Derby?”

Whoever the inventor was, kudos to them. This is a refreshing cocktail with just the right amount of floral sweetness from the honey syrup to complement the caramelly bourbon and bitter grapefruit juice. I highly recommend using fresh squeezed grapefruit juice over any cocktail style sugary concoction. I’ve seen some recipes call for sugar on the rim, I’d avoid it. You get enough sweetness from the honey and sugared rim just cheapens the whole experience.

    Ingredients
  • 1.5 oz Bourbon
  • .75 oz fresh squeezed grapefruit juice
  • .50 oz Honey Syrup (1:1)
  • Grapefruit for garnish
    Tools
  • Shaking Tins
  • Jigger
  • Fine Strainer
  • Hawthorne Strainers
  • Chilled Coupe
    Method
  1. Start by chilling down the glass with ice and soda water (you’re noticing a pattern here)
  2. Pour all the ingredients into the small Cheater Tin.
  3. Fill with ice.
  4. Cap it off with the large tin forming a nice seal and shake it like it stole something!
  5. When the tins are just about too cold to hold onto and there’s a proper layer of frost, crack open the tins with the liquid remaining in the large tin.
  6. Dump the ice water from the cocktail glass.
  7. Place the Hawthorne Straineron the tin to hold back the ice, and with your other hand hold the fine strainer over your cocktail glass. Pour the liquid through the fine strainer into the cocktail glass.
  8. You may have to give the fine strainer a few taps to filter all the liquid through, you don’t want to cheat yourself or your customer.
  9. Squeeze the grapefruit peel over the cocktail to get the nice bitter-sweet oils flowing over the top of the drink.
  10. Rim the glass with the grapefruit peel and hang it on the edge.
    11. Be Delighted!
